The Annual Reflection Guide for CEOs

Not a performance review. Not a goal-setting exercise. An invitation to slow down and ask the questions you have been too busy to ask.

I have been working with CEOs for years now, and there is something I have noticed. By the time December comes around, most of them are exhausted in a way that is hard to explain. Not just tired. Something deeper. The kind of depletion that comes from holding too much for too long.

The instinct at year's end is to push through. Finish strong. Maybe do a quick review of the numbers, set some goals, and collapse into the holidays. I understand that instinct. I have felt it myself.

But I want to offer you something different.

This guide is written the way I would talk to you if we were sitting together, somewhere quiet, with enough time and no agenda. Some of the questions might land. Others might not. That is fine. Take what is useful, leave the rest.

The only thing I would ask: do not rush. The things worth seeing tend to reveal themselves only when we stop moving long enough to notice them.

With warmth, Nikolas

Your 6-Part Journey

Sixteen questions across six territories. Each one invites you deeper into presence, clarity, and purpose.

01

Arriving

Before we go anywhere, we start here. How are you, actually? Not the polished answer. This opening pause invites you to notice how you're arriving: what your body is telling you, where you're holding tension, and what this year has cost you.

02

The Year That Was

Tell the honest story of your year. Not the investor update. Not the LinkedIn version. The version you'd tell an old friend over a long dinner. Every year has a shape: seasons of momentum, seasons of slog, moments that changed everything.

03

You

The company you're building cannot outgrow you. Your organization will eventually reflect who you are: your clarity and confusion, your courage and avoidance. This section invites an honest look at your energy, your patterns under pressure, and your blind spots.

04

The Company

At some point, your job stopped being about doing the work. It became about building the thing that does the work. What have you built? Where have you created real capability versus dependency? What's the gap between the culture you want and the culture that exists?

05

Your Life

You are not your company. You are a person with a body that needs rest, relationships that need tending, and a finite number of years to figure out what you actually want from this life. Who got your presence this year? When did you feel most alive?

06

Forward

Most annual reviews end with goals. This one ends with a quieter question: what wants to happen? Something that needs to end. Something that wants to begin. One thing, if you did it, that would change everything else.
